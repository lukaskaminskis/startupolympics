What is Startup Olympics?

Startup Olympics is a full day sports event that pools together Lithuanian startup ecosystem to challenge themselves in friendly competition, build closer relationships and salute the best teams.

For Startups

There are two ways for startups to participate in Startup Olympics:
1. 10 full startup teams (10 team members from each startup)
2. 2 mixed startup teams (2 members from 1 startup, 10 members in total)
We encourage every startup to prepare the most CREATIVE swag for your team (t-shirts, headbands, socks, flags etc.).
IMPORTANT: Building an open and integral startup community is of a great importance, therefore, every startup team will be joined by amotivated team member who will be dedicated to the team.

Registration deadline is August 10.

Startup Olympics rules

Startup Olympics is a sports tournament consisting of 9 different games. Each game will be organised in a single-elimination tournament or a knock-out system. The winners of each game will be saluted. Points of 3 leading startups of each game will be counted towards the final results where one single startup will become the Champion of Startup Olympics!

Table football

The match is played until one team scores 10 goals. Only one game is played. 2 players per team.

Table tennis

The match is played up to 21 points. Only one game is played. 1 player per team.

Badminton

The match is played up to 21 points. Only one game is played. 2 players per team.

Dodge Ball

Goal is to eliminate the opposing team by throwing one of balls to a competitor lower shoulders. 4 players per team.

Sumo

Players fight against each other wearing gorgeous SUMO costumes. 1 player per team.

Darts

Your goal is to be the first to reach zero from starting total of 301. 1 player per team.

Beer pong

Your goal is to throw a ping-pong ball into opponent‘s cup which has to be emptied. The winning team forces opponents to drink all their glasses. 2 players per team.

Flip the Cup

Your goal is to empty your cup, put it face up on the edge of the table and—using only one hand — flip it over so it lands face down. After flipping, the teammate must do the same with another cup. The wining team is first to flip 6 cups. 7 players per team.

"Tie a shoe"

Your goal is to run and tie a pair of shoes on the board, run back to your team and swop. The first team to tie all pairs of shoes wins. 7 players per team.
